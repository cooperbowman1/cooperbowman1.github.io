2026 & Design

January 5, 2026

As I step into 2026, I find myself reflecting on how far I've come in my design journey and where I want to go next. This year feels different—not because of any grand resolution, but because I'm finally understanding what it means to design with intention.

The past few years taught me that great design isn't just about making things look good. It's about solving problems, telling stories, and creating work that matters to the people who see it. Whether I'm designing a monument that honors someone's legacy or creating a sticker that brings a smile to someone's face, the underlying principle remains the same: design with purpose.

Looking back at 2024 and 2025, I've grown more confident in my craft. Working at Allen Monument Company has taught me precision and the weight of creating something permanent. Starting BOOPCOLLECTIVE© showed me the joy of creative freedom and building a brand from nothing. These experiences have shaped not just my skills, but my perspective on what design can be.

This year, I want to push myself further. I want to explore new techniques, experiment with different mediums, and continue learning from every project I take on. I'm particularly interested in diving deeper into print work and understanding the nuances that separate good design from exceptional design.

But beyond the technical growth, I want to stay true to what brought me here in the first place: a genuine love for creating. My father encouraged me to pursue creativity from a young age, and that foundation continues to guide me. His belief in me reminds me that design is more than a career—it's a way of seeing the world and making my mark on it.

2026 is about building on what I've learned while staying open to new possibilities. It's about maintaining the quality and precision I've developed while not being afraid to take creative risks. It's about honoring where I come from while carving out where I'm going.

Here's to another year of growth, creativity, and meaningful work.
